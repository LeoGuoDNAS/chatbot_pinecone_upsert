Internal Registry Custom Folder Pages (sys-folders-page)

Testing Your Registry Entries
The system does not verify the validity of registry entries. Be sure to test your registry entries after completing them to verify that they are functioning correctly.

Setting Values are Uppercase-Lowercase Sensitive:
The Setting Value is case-sensitive: be sure that you enter the value exactly as specified in the documentation. If the documentation specifies a value of true, do NOT enter True or TRUE.

Purpose:

This registry entry enables you to customize the individual folder tab pages referenced in Registry Entry for Custom Folders (sys-folders).

Usage:

This registry entry enables you to specify the functions, records and reports that appear on a tab page by indicating their position on a coordinate grid:


01       02         03        04        05        06
11        12        13        14        15        16
21        22        23        24        25        26
31        ....

SAM Pro will automatically include the appropriate button (report, viewer, or starter). You will also specify the labels that appear next to the buttons. Care must be taken to ensure that the labels do not overwrite one another or the buttons.

Arrows leading from one function to another (indicating order of operations) may be implemented via the lines option.

Important Fields:

Id:

sys-folders-page-pagename

Contents/Addtional Contents

pagename=name

this defines what will be referenced in the "sys-folders" as a topic page {ex. accountspayable}.

pagetitle=titlettext

where titletext will be the label of the topic page {ex. A/P }.

functions=functionid @ coordinate, anotherfunctionid @ coordinate

{ex. vaptrms @ 01, vvndr @ 11 }

Note: if more room is necessary for definition you can use the keys...
functions1, functions2, functions3, lines1, lines2, titles1, titles2,  etc. ...these definitions will be processed together.

titles=label @ coordinate anotherlabel @ coordinate

{ ex. AP&Terms@01,Vendors@11}

Note that the ampersand & forces a carriage return in label

lines=functionid >anotherfunctionid 

This specifies the arrows that you may draw from one function to the next.

{vaptrms>vvndr}
