Internal Bank Reconciliation Support Tips

The following suggestions may help you diagnosis problems if the Bank Reconcilition journal does not balance.

You first have to confirm that they ever had a ‘balanced’ starting point for Bank Recon -  and then find out which ‘month’ it FIRST went OUT of balance…

If they ‘don’t know’ then you have them do this month, by month, by month for the Bank Acct  (use filter to only show Bank Recon Jrnl’s for a given bank acct  & compare to Bank Recon Report for same month(s)).

Re:  Bank Recon report – page 1 is reading DIRECT from GL…..  They can’t use manual GL Journal Entry and expect BANK Recon to tie out.

When running Bank Recon report, for a Bank Acct  – date range = ACCOUNTING Period range - so I hope Acct Period = Statement Period

ALL Bank Transfers must be done via Bank Transfer Jrnl, not manual GL Journal Entry.

Find out what was done with any ‘outstanding checks’ prior to using bank rec and how handled in SamPro.

You can run the AP and AR GL Audit reports to find out if anything posted ‘out of balance’  –

And/or Research ‘debit’ entries to source ‘AP Journal’ to a BANK acct or ‘credit’ entries to source ‘AR Sales’ to a BANK Acct  to determine if VALID or why done

 

Run AP CHECK register (summary) for a given accounting period to confirm it matches what is in GL for same time frame/bank account.

What I find is that manual checks, EFT & CC/DC transactions (from AP) can create ‘problems’ prior to programming locking down the following:

In OLDER versions… 

They could add an AP entry – code as EFT to bank acct x then change this to bank acct y (which we no longer allow) – check viewer will only show it to bank acct x

In OLDER versions…

Problems with AP Journal “DELETE” mode  – sometimes it did NOT remove the Check in Check Viewer  or mark as check as VOID in check viewer

Other Considerations…  

Also make sure all Bank Deposit Jrnls = General Ledger source ‘ Deposit’ for Bank Account for same time frame

User can enter a AP Invoice and then later change it to an EFT entry –

This can be done if they elect to NOT enter a “EFT/Credit TRX id”  (I always let system ASSIGN this) – however the system will:

Always change the Check Date = ‘existing’ Effective date – so if  AP Invoice entered in May but paid by EFT in June AND you want the expense left in May but payment in June –

to do this, user must 1. Have MAY accounting period marked as CLOSED and 2. Change acct period & effect date to June date/June Acct Period

For customers who have upgraded from OLD versions….

We are no longer allow users to include ‘after the fact’ trx in Bank Recon – we used

To allow changes to a bank recon Jrnl once you’ve reconciled  - this is no longer allowed.

I had a customer doing this – when I put them on higher version of software, bank recon’s they thought were ‘reconciled’ now show as having an ‘Unreconciled balance’

Nothing we can do about this, except explain what they posted after the fact and why it show’s this.

 

General HELPFUL TOOLS….

In 7.3, we added to various viewers the ability to look up specific transactions for a given BANK ACCOUNT… or find “EFT or CrCard type transactions for a BANK ACCOUNT – I find these

Very useful in the CHECK VIEWER or AP JOURNAL and show customer how to pull these lists to help find info.

Very useful in the Bank Deposit Jrnl to ONLY show Bank Deposits for x bank acct

Same for Bank Transfers or Bank Recon Jrnls – use to ONLY show trx for x bank acct 

 

Prior to 7.3 (and sometimes in 7.3), I would use these extra ‘filters’ & show customer how to find info:

AP Journal Entry Viewer:

sql=apjrnl.gl_bnk_glaccnt_rn in (select glaccnt_rn from glaccnt where glaccnt_id = '1002')  à finds all MANUAL checks, EFT for bank acct 1002

sql=apjrnl_eft_crdt_trnsctn_id like '10888%'   à find a specific EFT/CC transaction  

In next id – type in DELETE à used to find all “deleted” transactions (VOIDED trx) for a specific bank acct

 

Check Viewer & Check Register Report  

sql=chck_type = 'void'

sql=chck_pymnt_type = 'eft'

sql=chck_eft_crdt_trnsctn_id like '13042%'   à find a specific EFT/CC transaction
