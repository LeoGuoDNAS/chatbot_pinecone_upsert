Report AP 1099

Purpose:

This function is used to print AP / Vendor 1099 NEC and MISC Forms.

For Tax Year 2020, the 1099 MISC will no longer be used to report information in Box 7 for Non Employee Compensation; it will continue to be used for reporting other information (along with other changes to the boxes listed on this form). The information previously reported on the 1099 MISC report in Box 7 will instead printed to a new form, the 1099 NEC. 

The SAMPro Report AP 1099 (RAP1099.rpt revised 2021-01-04 or later) supports SAMPro versions 8.2 and above. This report provides options that the user selects to run either report on either the 1099 MISC form or the 1099 NEC form.  A version for pre 8.2 is also available (RAP1099pre82.rpt revised 2020-01-07 or later).

1.  Vendor Records

The 1099 Misc. box must be checked on the Vendor record to be eligible for 1099’s.  The 1099 Category will determine whether they appear on the 1099 MISC or 1099 NEC form.  MOST VENDORS THAT WERE MARKED AS 1099 CATEGORY 7 MUST BE CHANGEED TO 1099 CATEGORY blank or 99 so that they will appear on the 1099 NEC form.

IMPORTANT CHANGE TO PREVIOUS YEARS:
Vendors that were previously set to 1099 Category 7 MUST have their 1099 Category changed to blank or 99 in order to appear on the revised 1099 NEC form.  If you have too many Vendor records to update manually contact Support for assistance. The form must be provided if at least $600 was paid to the Vendor in 2020.

File Form 1099-MISC, Miscellaneous Income, for each person in the course of your business to whom you have paid over $600 during the year.  The following 1099 Categories are supported in for 2020 1099 MISC.

2. Non-Intercompany Registries

The following registry entries must be completed before running this report either in the Registry viewer or in the Company Record / Company Record Registry Tab (Intercompany).

The Registry Entry Federal and State EIN must be set so that your company’s Federal Tax Id will print on the 1099.

The Registry Entry Vendor 1099 Report must be set for printing a second address line for your 1099 Vendors on the report.

The Registry Entry Company Address provides the information that is used to print your firm’s address on this form. This registry setting may be temporarily altered if you wish to include your firm’s telephone number on the form.

3. Intercompany Users

This report has been revised to use the Company record / Company Registry tab to specify the Federal EIN, Company Name and Address and other registry settings required for these reports. This will reduce the need for multiple registries entries by Company Security. The new Company Record overload logic supersedes Registry values when/if present.  The Registry Entry Vendor 1099 Report must be set for printing a second address line for your 1099 Vendors on the report.

4 Running and Aligning Your 1099’s

The Vendor Limit should be currently be set to conform to the 1099 requirements ($600 for 2020). The Start and End Dates should specify the calendar year reported on. 

New Field: 1099 Form
Select NEC or MISC depending on which form you are using.  Remember that Vendor’s 1099 Category must be set to blank or 99 to appear on the form 1099 NEC.

We suggest printing a 1099 NEC for one Vendor (by entering an Id in the Vendor range) on plain paper to check alignment.  The Top Margin field may be used to move the text down if it is printing too far up on the form.  The same process should also be followed for a 1099 MISC form.

Top Margin: This field may be used for alignment purposes if your 1099’s are printing too high on the form. Entering 1 or 2 (and tabbing out of the field) will move the report down by a line on the form. Entering -1 will move it up a line.

Filler Lines Adjustment: This field allowing the bottom form to be moved up or down relative to the top form. Entering 1 or 2 (and tabbing out of the field) will move the bottom from down. Entering -1 will move it up a line.

Tip: You can print your first alignment copies on plain paper, and then hold the plain paper copy up to the light with the printed form to check your alignment.

Registry Adjustments:  If the Filler Lines and Top Margin do not provide proper alignment, the following registry may be used when the 1099’s are being printed.  It must be disabled as soon as the 1099’s have been printed to prevent printing issues with other reports. It allow the report to be adjust to the left or right to make them fit in the form. We recommend limiting it to the individual that will be printing the 1099’s

Id: sys-printer-printername - - userid or sys-printer-printername-branchid-userid

Setting Key: marginoffset   Setting Value: x@y

Where x is the character offset (horizontal) and y is the line offset (vertical) from the upper left-hand corner of the page. If the text prints too far to the right on the form, the following registry will move it 3 characters to the left:

Setting Key: marginoffset Setting Value: -3@0

Note that the negative value chops off the left 3 columns of the report, which are blank in the case of the 1099.

If you had to change the Top Margin or Filler Lines Adjustment in your alignment run, be sure to set them when printing on your live forms.

5 Help Completing Vendor 1096

The Report Vendor Payment History (RVNDRPYMNTS.rpt) dated 01/30/2017 or later may be run to assist with the completion of the Federal Form 1096 which summarizes the 1099 forms for that year.  Note: if the 1099 Category is set to blank or 99, it indicates a 1099 NEC form.
