A known problem exists with a very particular series of events with Change Orders and Last Cost (see below).

We have decided that this example is too obscure to warrant the risks associated with either changing the query or changing the posting rules.  To see this specific failure, you must have multiple purchases of the same Item on the same date and 'change' the first purchase after the second purchase.  Finally, if you go one step further and close the second PO, the problem goes away.

Steps to Cause Problem

1. PO 10512 is entered in the system with 2 items 123127 for  50 @ 7.25 and 100-tip for 10 @ 10.50.
2. PRINT PO
3. Received Item 123127 on inventory receipt 10168
4. Partially invoice receipt for item 123127 and it is created in ap journal 10321. 
5. You get a price change on the 100-tip making it 10.75 each creating po 10512-01.
6. You reorder item 123127 on another purchase order 10513 and the cost has increased to 7.50.
7. Receive PO 10513 on inventory receipts journal 10169.
8. Create AP journal 10322 to pay for po 10513.
9. Here is where the bug comes in. In Laura's data, put a closed date on change order 10512-01. You will find that it is writing a line to imhstry for a purchase for the item that was received in full (123127)and it is changing the last cost.  It is writing a qty on order and a qty order."
